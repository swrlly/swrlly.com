We are now in a position to talk about the integral.

First, let's discover what the formula for the integral depends on.

Before we do so, we need to discuss sigma notation.

Suppose you would like to add \[1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + \cdots + 100\] You can compactly represent this sum in sigma notation: \[1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + \cdots + 100 = \sum^{100}_{i = 1} i\]

  • The $i$ below the sigma is called the index.
  • The number above the sigma is where $i$ should stop.
